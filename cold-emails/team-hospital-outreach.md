## Cross-Template Design Principles

| Design Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Pain point opens every email | Pain-point emails perform 202% better than generic intros |
| Credibility in sentence 2, not sentence 1 | Reader's attention engaged before evaluating sender |
| "Berkeley Neuroscience, AI Fellow at Define Ventures" | Compact (9 words), signals both technical depth and healthcare investing credibility |
| Differentiated value language per persona | CFO: dollars. CMO: quality scores. VBC: domain optimization. Surgical: workflow |
| CTA matched to decision style | CFO/VBC: calculator (data-driven). CMO: quality walkthrough. Surgical: short demo |
| 120-170 words per email | Hospital execs are swamped -- respect their time |
| No tech stack mentions | Hospital execs don't care about Claude, Tavus, or ElevenLabs |
| No attachments, no competitor comparisons | Best practice from healthcare B2B cold email research |
| Single CTA per email | Multiple CTAs reduce conversion |

## Send Timing Best Practices
- **Best days:** Tuesday or Thursday (midweek)
- **Best times:** 10-11 AM or 2 PM
- **Follow-up:** First follow-up adds 40-50% more replies. Space 2-3 days apart. Add new value each time (don't re-send same email). Max 3-4 follow-ups.
